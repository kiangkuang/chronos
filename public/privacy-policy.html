Privacy Policy for Chronos

Last updated: December 2024

Introduction

Chronos ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our calendar application that integrates with Google Calendar.

Information We Collect

Google Calendar Data

When you sign in to Chronos using your Google account, we may access and collect the following information from your Google Calendar:

  • Calendar Events: Event titles, descriptions, start times, end times, and attendee information
  • Calendar Settings: Your primary calendar and associated calendar data
  • Authentication Tokens: Temporary access tokens to read your calendar data

Technical Information

We may also collect:

  • Session Storage: Authentication tokens and user preferences stored locally in your browser
  • Usage Data: How you interact with the application (features used, pages visited)
  • Device Information: Browser type, operating system, and device identifiers

How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • Calendar Integration: To display and manage your calendar events within the Chronos application
  • Work Hours Calculation: To calculate and display your work hours based on selected events
  • User Experience: To provide personalized calendar views and improve application functionality
  • Authentication: To verify your identity and maintain secure access to your calendar data

Data Storage and Security

Local Storage

  • Authentication tokens are stored locally in your browser's session storage
  • User preferences and settings are stored locally
  • No calendar data is permanently stored on our servers

Data Security

  • We use industry-standard security measures to protect your information
  • All communication with Google APIs is encrypted using HTTPS
  • Authentication tokens are automatically managed and expire according to Google's security policies

Third-Party Services

Google Calendar API

  • Chronos integrates with Google Calendar API to access your calendar data
  • Your use of Google Calendar is subject to Google's Privacy Policy
  • We only request read-only access to your calendar data
  • You can revoke access to your Google Calendar at any time through your Google account settings

Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personal information to third parties, except:

  • Google Services: As necessary to provide calendar integration functionality
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law or to protect our rights and safety
  • Service Providers: To trusted third parties who assist us in operating our application (if any)

Your Rights and Choices

Access and Control

  • Revoke Access: You can revoke Chronos's access to your Google Calendar at any time
  • Delete Data: Clear your browser's session storage to remove locally stored data
  • Opt Out: Stop using the application to discontinue data collection

Google Account Settings

  • Manage your Google Calendar sharing settings through your Google account
  • Control which calendars are accessible to third-party applications
  • Review and manage your Google account's connected applications

Data Retention

  • Session Data: Authentication tokens and session data are automatically cleared when you sign out or close your browser
  • Local Storage: User preferences may persist until you clear your browser data
  • No Server Storage: We do not permanently store your calendar data on our servers

Children's Privacy

Chronos is not intended for use by children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your own. By using Chronos, you consent to such transfers and processing.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by:

  • Posting the new Privacy Policy on this page
  • Updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy

California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Request disclosure of personal information we collect
  • Request deletion of your personal information
  • Opt out of the sale of personal information (we do not sell personal information)

European Privacy Rights (GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Rectify inaccurate personal data
  • Erase your personal data
  • Restrict processing of your personal data
  • Data portability
  • Object to processing of your personal data

Consent

By using Chronos, you consent to the collection and use of your information as described in this Privacy Policy.
